Puakenikeni

Puakenikeni is a fragrant flower often used in Hawaiian leis. Its name means “ten-cent flower” because it was once sold for ten cents each. The blossoms start white and turn orange as they age.

Pikake

Pikake, also known as jasmine, is a small white flower with a sweet scent. It is a favorite for making leis and symbolizes love and romance in Hawaiian culture.

Back

Bird of Paradise

The Bird of Paradise has bright orange and blue petals that look like a tropical bird in flight. It represents joy and paradise, and it grows well in warm climates.

Back

Hibiscus

The Hibiscus is Hawaii’s state flower. It comes in many colors and has large, beautiful petals. It symbolizes delicate beauty and is often worn behind the ear.

Back

Naupaka

Naupaka is a unique flower that looks like half a blossom. One type grows near the beach and another in the mountains. Legend says they are two lovers separated forever, each half waiting for the other.

Back

Plumeria

Plumeria flowers are known for their soft pink petals with a red or yellow center. They are often used in leis and symbolize positivity, new beginnings, and beauty.
